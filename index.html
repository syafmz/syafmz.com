Who am I?

My name is Nurul Syafiqah Bte Mohamad Zailani. I'm a year old 2D Generalist based in Singapore.

I am currently a final year undergrad of DigiPen Institute of Technology Singapore majoring in Digital Art and Animation.

My field of work is in digital arts. I do mostly illustration for print media and develop user ideas on screen employing interactive and visual design. To me, user experience is everything!

2D Generalist

The nature of my studies has taught me a vast variety of 2D skills. This includes UI/UX design, graphic design, storyboarding, animatics, concept art, asset creation, illustration and matte painting.

I leverage upon these unique skill set to value add and contribute on business objectives. To continue growing as an artist, I am self-motivated to keep learning and jump on opportunities that will allow me to do so.

Educator

Apart from being a full-time undergrad, I spend a good deal of time working as a freelance art teacher in Artary. I teach students how to paint, draw, and create art using various mediums.

I believe in the phrase,

In learning you will teach, and in teaching you will learn.

Balancing both creating and teaching art have been a source of absolute joy for me. Not only am I able to hone my craft as an artist, but I see every child as an opportunity to influence art in society.

Gamer

When I'm not working, I enjoy gaming with my friends and have recently built myself a custom gaming PC to play some of the newest releases.

Some titles I spent countless hours on are Defense of the Ancients (DotA), Dota Underlords, Don't Starve Together, Overcooked! and many more.

Cat-Lover

If you're also a cat lover, then we're already friends!

My love for fluffy feline stretch back to my early childhood when I rescued a cat trapped in a fire hose reel cabinet. Since then, I took it upon myself to care for them and have personally rescued several more lost and distressed cats.

Being around them calms me and I instantly feel at peace even in the worst days. Their curiosity, playfulness, and independence, pretty much shaped me into the person I am today.
